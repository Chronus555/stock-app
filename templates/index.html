About the Adaptive Entropy Momentum Index (AEMI)

The Problem

Classic momentum indicators (MACD, RSI) treat all market regimes equally. But markets oscillate between predictable trending phases and random efficient phases. Using the same signal in both regimes generates massive false positives.

The Solution

AEMI computes the Shannon entropy of the rolling log-return distribution to measure how "random" the market is right now. A predictability weight W = 1 − H_norm then scales the momentum signal — shrinking it toward zero during high-entropy (chaotic) periods.

The Formula

H    = -Sum p(ri) * log2 p(ri)
W    = 1 - H / log2(n_bins)
M    = EMA_10 / EMA_30 - 1
V    = tanh(Vol / EMA_Vol - 1)
AEMI = M * W * (1 + 0.35*V)

Why This Works

Renaissance Technologies famously exploited market inefficiencies using mathematical models. The key insight is that market efficiency is not constant — it fluctuates. During periods of herding, panic, or momentum cascades, prices become temporarily predictable. AEMI detects these windows by measuring the information entropy of the return distribution.

  • Low entropy = returns are clustered/trending = high predictability = strong AEMI signal
  • High entropy = returns are dispersed/random = low predictability = AEMI near zero
  • Volume confirmation = moves backed by volume participation are more reliable

Model Architecture

The prediction engine uses an ensemble of Random Forest (300 trees) and Gradient Boosting (150 estimators), combining their probability outputs. Features include 18+ technical indicators, 20 lag features, and 6 rolling statistics. All evaluated with proper time-series cross-validation to prevent lookahead bias.
